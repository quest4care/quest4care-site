FoundationReady · Assessment Portal

Build the Foundation
Funders Require.

FoundationReady™ is a structured capacity assessment built on the QCLS CARES Framework. It delivers scored results with written justification tied to specific evidence — not generic observations. Every finding is clearly labeled so your team knows exactly where you stand and why.

What FoundationReady™ Delivers

Every assessment produces a CARES score — a numeric measure of organizational maturity across five domains — and a stage determination from Stage 1 Emerging through Stage 4 Grant Ready. Each finding is labeled Evidence-Based, Probable, or Speculative, and every conclusion is tied to specific organizational evidence you provide during the assessment process.

The result is a complete picture of your organization's infrastructure maturity: where it is strong, where it is fragile, where funders will have questions, and what to address first. Not a checklist. A diagnostic with a roadmap.

FoundationReady™ findings feed directly into Phorsyte™ foresight mapping and theShift™ action planning — the assessment is the foundation every next step is built on.

Who FoundationReady™ serves: Nonprofits at any stage — from organizations just receiving their 501(c)(3) determination to established organizations preparing for major funder relationships. Funders who want an independent assessment of an organization before investing. Boards seeking an honest picture of organizational health.

Phorsyte · Foresight Portal

See Tomorrow.
Act Today.

Phorsyte™ maps the external environment your organization is navigating — funding chains, governance networks, ecosystem opportunities and threats — and projects where your organization is likely heading based on evidence, not aspiration.

What Phorsyte™ Reveals

Most organizations know who their funders are. Phorsyte™ goes three layers deeper — mapping your funders, their funders, and their funders' funders. Each relationship receives a rating: STABLE, WATCH, FRAGILE, or UNKNOWN. A grant that looks secure on the surface may be one leadership change or one policy shift away from disappearing. Phorsyte™ surfaces that risk before it becomes a crisis.

Phorsyte™ also maps governance networks — board member overlaps with other organizations and funders, funder-board interlocks that create conflicts of interest, vendor relationships that may not be visible in a standard 990 review. The Governance Transparency Score produced by Phorsyte™ gives funders and boards an honest picture of organizational independence.

The output is a Phorsyte™ Ecosystem Opportunity Map — a client-specific visual showing where your organization sits in the community ecosystem, which relationships are strengthening it, which are threatening it, and where the opportunities are that no individual organization can see alone.

Phorsyte™ follows FoundationReady™. The assessment tells you where you are. Phorsyte™ tells you where you are likely going. Together they give your organization the clearest picture of its situation available anywhere in Henry and Madison County.

theShift · Action Portal

Strategic Human Impact
Focused Transformation.

theShift™ converts FoundationReady™ assessment findings and Phorsyte™ foresight intelligence into a prioritized 90-day action plan calibrated to your actual organizational capacity — not your aspirational capacity. Every recommendation is tied to evidence. Every step is sequenced to what your organization can actually execute.

What theShift™ Produces

Most strategic plans fail because they are built for an organization that does not yet exist — staffed, funded, and capacitated at a level the organization hopes to reach, not where it actually is. theShift™ is different. Every recommendation is calibrated to the CARES stage determined by FoundationReady™ and weighted by the trajectory intelligence produced by Phorsyte™.

The output is an Action Matrix — four quadrants that answer the most important question any organization can ask: of everything we could do, what should we do first? Do First, Plan and Execute, Delegate, and Eliminate. The matrix is built from evidence, not consensus or aspiration.

theShift™ plans are living documents. As the organization executes the 90-day plan, FoundationReady™ rescores the relevant domains, Phorsyte™ updates the trajectory, and theShift™ recalibrates the next cycle. The system never stops learning. The organization never stops improving.

Request a theShift™ Plan
Quadrant 1

Do First

High impact, immediately executable. Actions your organization has the capacity to complete now that will produce the greatest return on the investment of attention.

Quadrant 2

Plan & Execute

High impact, requires preparation. Actions worth doing that need resources, planning, or capacity building before execution begins.

Quadrant 3

Delegate

Lower impact, can be handled by others. Actions that need to happen but do not require leadership attention. Assign, automate, or partner.

Quadrant 4

Eliminate

Low impact, consuming capacity. Activities and commitments that are not producing value relative to what they cost. Stop doing them.

qOHR · Intelligence Core

QuestCareOrganizational Human Reality.

qOHR™ is the intelligence layer beneath the entire UnoSphere™. It is not a portal any constituent accesses directly. It is the system that connects every data point across every portal into patterns no individual organization can see alone.

Every FoundationReady™ assessment completed, every OurWalk™ case closed as Access Confirmed, every weCARES™ field observation submitted, every Phorsyte™ funding chain analyzed, every AskQCLS™ inquiry answered — all of it flows into qOHR™ and becomes pattern intelligence.

The community loop that qOHR™ powers: Community Reality → Data → qOHR™ → Intelligence → theShift™ → Community Change → Community Reality. The loop never stops. The system never stops learning. Every cycle makes the intelligence more precise and the community outcomes more measurable.

qOHR™ is QCLS staff only. The patterns and correlations qOHR™ generates are used to improve services, inform funder conversations, and strengthen the community ecosystem — never disclosed to individual organizations or shared in ways that identify specific constituents.

Compound Funding Risk Detection

When multiple nonprofits in the ecosystem share a fragile funder, qOHR™ identifies the compound risk before it becomes a community-wide crisis.

Service Desert Identification

Aggregate OurWalk™ navigation data reveals where service demand is unmet — geographic gaps, service category gaps, population gaps that no individual provider can see from their position.

Governance Concentration Patterns

Across all Phorsyte™ assessments, qOHR™ maps governance concentration in the community ecosystem — where power is concentrated, where independence is at risk, where the community is vulnerable.

Community Demand Trends

Field intelligence from weCARES™ combined with navigation outcomes from OurWalk™ generates a real-time picture of community need that no survey, no 990, and no annual report can replicate.

About QCLS

Built on Nearly
Two Decades of
Systems Experience.

QuestCare LifeStyle Solutions was founded by MarQuest Higgins, who brings nearly two decades of operational leadership in Medicaid-funded disability services — including compliance, financial controls, board governance, program documentation, and outcome measurement from the inside.

QCLS received its 501(c)(3) determination on January 24, 2025. We are incorporated in Indiana, operating with a documented strategic plan, an independent governing board, and a clear three-pillar mission.

We are not consultants. We are a community systems organization — building the accountability infrastructure that enables nonprofits to earn trust, access funding, and deliver measurable outcomes.

Why infrastructure instead of direct service. Henry and Madison County already have providers. What they do not have is a system that connects them — that assesses nonprofit capacity honestly, maps funding fragility before it becomes a crisis, navigates individuals to confirmed access rather than a referral, and generates the intelligence that makes every existing service more effective. QCLS was built to be that system.

What nearly two decades actually means. MarQuest Higgins built and operated Medicaid-funded disability service systems from the inside — writing the compliance documentation, building the financial controls, sitting in the board meetings, measuring the outcomes, and watching what happened when the infrastructure was not there. QCLS is not built on theory. It is built on the specific failures and successes of organizations that served this community before QCLS existed.

The gap no one else was filling. Community foundations in Henry and Madison County hold more than $88 million in combined assets. The nonprofits they fund — many of them — operate without independent audits, without logic models, without board conflict of interest policies, without reserves. The funders know this. The nonprofits know this. No one had built the infrastructure to close the gap. QCLS did.

Three Strategic Pillars

Pillar 01

Community Access Navigation

Most navigation systems hand people a list of phone numbers and call it a referral. QCLS does not. OurWalk™ assigns a human navigator who stays with the case from intake to Access Confirmed — the only metric that closes a case. If an individual encounters a barrier, the navigator removes it. If a provider cannot deliver, the navigator finds one who can. The case is never closed until the person actually receives the service they need.

Access Confirmed is not a referral milestone. It is proof that the system worked.

Pillar 02

Nonprofit Capacity & Intelligence

Nonprofits in Henry and Madison County serve the most vulnerable populations in the region — and most of them operate without the governance infrastructure, financial transparency, or strategic intelligence funders increasingly require. QCLS closes that gap through three connected portals. FoundationReady™ delivers an honest, evidence-based picture of where an organization stands today. Phorsyte™ maps where it is likely heading — funding chain stability, governance risks, ecosystem opportunities. theShift™ converts that intelligence into a 90-day action plan calibrated to what the organization can actually execute, not what it aspires to.

The three portals are designed to be used together. Each one feeds the next. The result is the clearest picture of organizational health available anywhere in this region.

Pillar 03

Phorsyte™ & Ecosystem Accountability

The most important intelligence about a community ecosystem cannot be seen from inside any single organization. It only becomes visible when data from every organization — assessments, navigation outcomes, field observations, funder relationships, governance structures — is connected into a single picture. qOHR™, the QuestCare Organizational Human Reality layer, is where that picture is built. It identifies compound funding fragility before it becomes a community-wide crisis. It maps service deserts no individual provider can see from their position. It surfaces governance concentration patterns that put the entire ecosystem at risk. It tracks community demand trends in real time through weCARES™ field intelligence.

qOHR™ is never marketed to constituents. Its intelligence is used to improve every service QCLS delivers — and to give funders and community leaders a level of ecosystem visibility that has never existed in Henry and Madison County before.
